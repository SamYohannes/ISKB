Salzburg, Austria
The city with breathtaking views of the Eastern Alps


What to do in Salzburg:

Salzburg Fortress
Otherwise known as Hohensalzburg Castle, this grand Fortress looms over the Festungsberg Hill and has been a major power hive for the rulers of Salzburg since the 1000’s.
Inside are many rooms and wings to explore containing various artefacts and traditional decor; the Powder Room, Chapel and Golden Hall are just a few of the wonderful interiors that await inside
Mozart's Birthplace
The influence and fame of Mozart is evident in Salzburg. Born in a small house originally, the building was reconstructed as closely as possible to the original design after its destruction during World War II and is now open as a museum. A certified place to visit for music lovers
Mirabell Palace and Gardens
First established in 1606, this palace is recognised as a cultural heritage monument.As well as the striking Marble Hall which is a venue for Palace Concerts, the Palace also has a wondrous set of gardens that can be explored at your leisure.If you enjoy peaceful strolls around nature and being in the outdoors, the palace is a brilliant place to visit



Where to stay in Salzburg:

MEININGER Hotel Salzburg City Centre
Like the title quotes, the centre is where you'll find this well organised, well cleaned sizeable bedrooms. Comes with free breakfast and great for families or those travelling on a budget
Das Gruene Bio-Hotel zur Post
This hotel describes itself as a “home away from home.” Das Gruene Bio-Hotel zur Post aims to make one's stay as relaxing and enjoyable as possible. Given the close proximity of popular landmarks, such as MoenchsbergAufzug and Mozart's Birthplace , guests can easily experience some of Salzburg's most well known attractions. And, you guessed it, a stupendious value for money
Hotel Max 70
A little on the pricier side, this hotel is a hidden gem located slightly further from the city centre but no fear, its a short walk to the bus stop and only a 15 min bus ride to town.
